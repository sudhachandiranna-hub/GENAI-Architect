Introduction to RPA — The Bridge to AI Automation

Before we touch AI-based automation, we need to understand where automation came from — RPA, PyAutoGUI, and Playwright. Plus a generous helping of mentor wisdom, served with a biryani analogy.

6-Week Journey 6.67% complete

Today's Core Topic — RPA

01
RPA = Robotic Process Automation
A framework built on Python. Mandatory to understand before entering AI-based automation — this is the foundation everything else builds on.
02
PyAutoGUI — UI Automation Framework
It's a script — a set of instructions that execute one after another. It takes over your mouse and keyboard to perform tasks automatically.
03
Before AI, Automation Meant Manual Scripting
Almost all automation — basic mouse operations, keyboard functions — was done using Python scripts. No AI involved. Just precise, hardcoded instructions.
04
Playwright — Advanced Browser Automation
An advanced version of Selenium, used for web browser-based automation: LinkedIn post automation and web data scraping.
🔄 Why Selenium Was Removed From the Course

A member asked why earlier Selenium videos are gone. Thiru clarified: based on a lot of cohort feedback, people were getting confused having both frameworks taught side by side — so Selenium was removed and the course now focuses purely on Playwright, its more advanced successor. Whenever content is updated, an email notification goes out to all previous batches too — not just the current one.

🍛 Thiru's Analogy — What Is a Framework?

To explain "what is a framework," Thiru used a biryani analogy — there are two ways to make biryani:

🌶️ From Scratch
Grind your own fresh masala, source all raw materials individually, build the dish from zero.
= Writing raw code, no framework
📦 Ready-Made Masala
Use a pre-made biryani masala mix — proven, packaged, ready to go.
= Using a framework (PyAutoGUI, Playwright)

A framework is the ready-made masala of programming — pre-built, tested structure so you don't reinvent the wheel every time.

🌟 Mentor Wisdom — From Thiru

🍲
Learn Through What You Love

Thiru shared a personal habit: he loves food, and he relates everything he learns to food analogies. When learning something new, he often asks ChatGPT to explain it using food/recipe comparisons — it's how his mind retains concepts. He calls it a kind of psychology. His suggestion to the cohort: find what you love, and relate new learning to it. That's how you master anything.

— Thiru, on his personal learning method

🔁
Make New Mistakes Every Day

"I have made many mistakes — make new mistakes every day." Don't repeat old mistakes, but don't fear new ones either. By solving new mistakes, you learn something new every single day. That's the actual engine of growth.

— Thiru's mantra on learning through failure

⚡
Learn and Implement — Not Learn, Then Implement

In this AI era, the people who win are the ones who focus more on implementation. Don't wait to learn everything before you start building. Do and learn — not learn and do. Implementation itself is the fastest teacher.

— Thiru's implementation-first philosophy

🤖
Think of a World Without AI

"In 2019, when I learned all this, it took me 6 months — with no ChatGPT to guide me, and a lot of mistakes along the way. What you all are learning in a single day today is only possible because of AI." Thiru asked the cohort to genuinely picture a world without ChatGPT or Claude — and realize how much leverage we have right now. Fluid intelligence — adapting and learning on the fly with AI as a partner — is becoming the real skill of this era.

— Thiru, comparing his 2019 learning journey to today
